Play

Lexicon Standoff

Starting a Game

A Player (Host) starts a game on a phone, tablet, or computer by visiting lexiconstandoff.com. The Host shares the gamesecret with other Players, who join the game on their phone, tablet, or computer. Once all Players are in the lobby, the Host starts the first Round.

Gameplay

Players are assigned a role of Agent or Imposter at the beginning of each Round. Role Distribution is as close to 2 Agents to 1 Imposter as possible.

A Round is split into four stages:

  • Turns: Players take turns saying a word for two given Topics. Agents have better intel than Imposters.
  • Reveal: The two Topics are revealed to all Players.
  • Actions: Alive Players vote who to kill, dead Players elect to confuse other Players.
  • Results: Players are killed, scores are displayed.

Turns

There are two Topics generated for the Round (eg. “Goose” & “Spider”). Agents will only see the two Topics on their turn. Imposters will see both in addition to one extra. They will not know which are the real Topics and which are fake.

  • Agents will see “Goose” & “Spider”
  • Imposters will see “Goose”, “Mountain”, “Spider”, “Computer”

On an alive Player's turn, they must say one word they associate with both Topics.

  • A good word for “Goose” & “Spider” would be “Web” since geese have webbed feet and spiders make webs.
  • Words cannot be repeated by other Players during a set of Turns.

Turns should be completely silent aside from Players saying their word. After every living Player says their word, the Host moves the Game to the Reveal stage.

* If there are at least five Players
The Imposters and dead Agents will see who are Imposters on their device.

* If there are at least three sets of Turns
Players may be confused by dead Players, in which case they will have an extra fake Topic—the Topic of Confusion. There will be one randomly-selected Player called the Key Master who will know what the Topic of Confusion is.

Reveal

All Players will be see the real two Topics on their device. They can ask other Players why they chose their word.

Agents are trying to figure out who are Imposters and the Imposters are trying to turn Agents against each other. Everyone is trying to appear confident in their decision. Players may give any explanation as to why they chose their word, even if it is a lie. This is not about who chose the best word.

* If there are at least five Players
The Imposters and dead Agents will see who are Imposters on their device.

* If there are at least three sets of Turns
Players that were confused by other Players may explain their choice relative to the Topic of Confusion. The Key Master can be alive or dead, but will have known the Topic of Confusion and can verify confused Players' reasoning. However, any Player can claim to have been the Key Master and the Key Master is allowed to lie about the Topic of Confusion.

Actions

Every living Player votes on their device for who they want to kill. Every dead Player selects someone to confuse. The next Round, confused Players will have an extra fake Topic. After every Player submits their action, the Round will progress on its own.

* If there are two living Players left in the Round
Dead Players will be voting to kill instead of confuse.

Results

Players receiving the most votes are killed, even when there is a tie. The Round is over when only Imposters remain, only Agents remain, or everyone dies.

If the Round is still active:

  • Players keep their role for the next round so they should not reveal it at this time.
  • Two new Topics are generated and the game will start another Turns stage for the survivors.
  • The next alive Player clockwise of the Host goes first. Rotate this first turn every Round.

If the Round is over:

  • Imposters are revealed to all Players.
  • Game Standings will be displayed
  • The Host can start a new Round which will assign new roles for each Player, or they can end the Game.

Scoring

Surviving Imposters receive two points and surviving Agents receive one point.

If the Round is over:

If one group wins, all Players on that team receive three points. For example:

  • If two of three Agents survive, all three Agents receive three additional points
  • If two of three Imposters survive, all three Imposters receive three additional points

If everyone dies, no one receives the extra points.

Strategy

Agents want to be discreet in their selection so as not to make it obvious to the Imposters what the two Topics are, but also specific enough so that fellow Agents will trust them.

Imposters can use Agents’ word choices to try and figure out which two Topics are the real Topics. They should also be abstract enough with their word so that they can defend their choice when the two Topics are revealed.

* If there are at least three sets of Turns
Any Player can claim to have been confused by the Topic of Confusion.

Any Player can claim to have been the Key Master for the Round and can claim the Topic of Confusion was anything that makes another Player look bad or to help Players trust another.

If you are confused by another Player, but are also the Key Master, you can use your knowledge of the Topic of Confusion to help determine what the two Topics are for the Round.
